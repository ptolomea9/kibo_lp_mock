2 — Why B2B Commerce First

This Is the Highest-Impact Keyword Cluster

We started with the B2B Commerce cluster for three reasons:

  1. Most keywords. This cluster covers 15–20 keywords — the largest single group in the campaign. It includes terms like b2b commerce platform (1,792 impressions), best b2b ecommerce platforms (607), b2b commerce software (563), and b2b ecommerce platforms (557).
  2. Highest combined impression volume. The B2B General ad group has the highest total historical impressions (~5,000+) across all non-brand ad groups.
  3. Most misaligned keywords. Four keywords in this cluster (unified commerce platform, digital commerce solutions, b2b trade portal, b2b dealer portal) currently point to the homepage — a page that serves every audience and can't be optimized for any single keyword. These are the biggest mismatch in the account.
Bottom line: Fixing one page here touches more keywords, more impressions, and the worst landing page misalignments in the account. If it works, we replicate for OMS, Wholesale, and Inventory clusters.

4 — What You're Doing Well

Strengths to Build On

After crawling all six pages, there's a lot of strong foundation to work with:

  • Ad relevance is excellent. 87% of keywords are rated Above Average — the ad copy and keyword-to-ad alignment is strong across the board.
  • PPC-specific pages already exist. /ppc/wholesale/, /ppc/distributor/, and /ppc/manufacturing/ show the team already knows paid traffic needs different treatment.
  • Embedded forms exist on multiple pages. All three /ppc/ pages have HubSpot form embeds, and even /solutions/b2b/ and /platform/order-management/ have forms. The conversion mechanism is there.
  • Forrester TEI data is being used. /solutions/b2b/ already cites 296% revenue increase, 167% ROI, and 67% fewer returns from the Forrester study. These are powerful proof points.
  • Comparison tables and problem/solution content exist. /solutions/b2b/, /platform/order-management/, and even some /ppc/ pages have comparison table structures and before/after framing.
  • Customer testimonials are present. Fortis Life Sciences is quoted across all three /ppc/ pages, and /solutions/b2b/ references a CTO testimonial.
  • Product positioning is clear. "Unified Commerce + OMS on one data model" is genuinely unique in the market. The messaging is right.
  • Content depth is an SEO asset. The 3,500–4,200 word pages are valuable for organic search. The issue is using them for paid traffic where visitors need immediate relevance.

7 — What Competitors Do

How the Market Handles PPC Landing Pages

BigCommerceDedicated /dm/ demo pages with partially stripped navigation. Forms link to separate pages rather than being embedded. Lightweight builds for PPC traffic.
Sana CommerceEmbedded Marketo forms with strong quantified social proof ("80% adoption", "30% revenue growth"). Full site navigation still present on all pages.
commercetoolsUses product pages with full navigation. No embedded forms. Relies on 3,000+ multi-channel ads and ABM targeting via 6sense to compensate.
Salesforce Commerce CloudDedicated form pages at /form/ paths. Very heavy enterprise CMS. Forms live on separate pages from product content.
Where we stand out: No competitor PPC landing page has an ROI calculator, FAQ schema, or truly stripped navigation. Those three features are differentiators not just vs. current Kibo pages, but vs. the entire competitive set.
