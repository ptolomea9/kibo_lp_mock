The Audit

We Crawled Every Page the Non-Brand Ads Drive To

We pulled Quality Score data on all active non-brand keywords, identified the 6 landing pages receiving paid traffic, and crawled each one — including the three dedicated /ppc/ pages — to assess content alignment, conversion paths, and page structure against B2B PPC best practices.

The core finding: Ad copy and keyword alignment are in good shape — 87% of keywords score Above Average for Ad Relevance. The gap is the landing pages themselves: full site navigation (15+ exit paths), content built for organic education rather than paid conversion, and no single clear funnel toward a form fill.
The Solution

A Purpose-Built PPC Landing Page for B2B Commerce

We built a dedicated landing page for the B2B Commerce keyword cluster — the largest keyword group in the campaign (~20 keywords, 5,000+ impressions). Here's what changed versus the existing best PPC page (/ppc/wholesale/):

× Current State

  • Full site nav with dropdown menus
  • Same Fortis quote on all 3 /ppc/ pages
  • Stats section shows 0% (broken render)
  • No interactive conversion tools
  • No FAQ or schema markup
  • 500KB–1.5MB Elementor page weight

✓ What We Built

  • Stripped nav: logo, phone, single CTA
  • 4 distinct testimonials with real metrics
  • Forrester-sourced stats (296% rev, 167% ROI)
  • Interactive ROI calculator (TEI-based)
  • 6 FAQs with JSON-LD rich snippet schema
  • ~109KB purpose-built page

The Impact

Why This Should Move Quality Score

Landing Page Experience is the only QS sub-component still rated Below Average across every keyword. Ad Relevance and Expected CTR are already in good shape. Fixing the remaining weak link has the highest ROI:

3.1 → 5–6

What a QS jump from 3 to 6 means: ~20% lower CPCs at the same ad position. For a campaign spending $10K/month, that's $2K/month in savings — or the same budget buying 25% more clicks. If it works here, we replicate the template for OMS, Wholesale, and Inventory clusters.
